Our Story

Built with love,
driven with purpose

We're a grassroots time attack team with two Mazdas, zero excuses, and a lot of heart. A classic 1992 Miata and a factory-turbo 2004 Mazdaspeed — different machines, same mission.

Racing is our happy place. The track is where the noise of life fades out and it's just you, the car, and the road ahead. We don't chase championships — we chase good laps, good memories, and good people.

The name Littlefoot is a nod to two things: the lovable dinosaur from the movies, and a son we loved and lost — whose memory leaves a mark on everything we do.

💛 Every lap we run carries a little extra meaning. This team exists because someone we loved reminds us how precious and fragile life is — and how important it is to live it fully. We race in his honor.

Our Why

Why We Race

Some racing teams chase trophies. Some chase lap records. We started Littlefoot Racing chasing something different — a way to move through grief, to celebrate a life, and to turn a broken heart into something that goes fast around corners.

The idea started simply: get a car, get on a track, feel alive. But the deeper we got into it, the more it became about something bigger than motorsport. It became about honoring a kid who never got to grow up, by doing something he would have thought was the coolest thing ever.

His name lives in the team name. His spirit lives in the way we approach this — with joy, with stubbornness, with a willingness to look ridiculous in a parking lot at 7am in a 30-year-old Japanese sports car. We do this because of him. For him. With him.

🦶
The name Littlefoot comes from two places: the beloved dinosaur from the movie, and a son named Littlefoot who we never got to bring home. He was with us only briefly, but he is with us always. This team is our way of making sure his memory never fades.
We don't race to win. We race to remember — to honor a son we loved, to live fully in his name, and to make sure he is never forgotten.
🏁 First Lap Dedication
When this car crosses a timing line for the first time, that lap belongs to him. Every lap after that too.
— Littlefoot Racing, founded 2025
